I am developing a disposable hot-beverage cup called SafeTemp™ that has a built-in thermometer strip down the side. The strip is a non-toxic, medical-grade thermochromic indicator that changes color based on the temperature of the liquid inside: green for safe to drink, yellow for caution, red for too hot. The temperature scale on the strip is anchored to the 2008 Brown and Diller study showing the optimal serving range for hot beverages is 125 to 136 degrees Fahrenheit (54 to 60 Celsius), with a preferred value at 136 degrees Fahrenheit (57.8 Celsius). The cup body uses standard food-grade paper materials in small, medium, and large sizes. Primary customers are parents and caregivers buying for their children, where accidental burns are the most acute problem. Secondary customers are coffee shops, cafes, quick-service food chains, schools, daycares, and eldercare facilities that face liability exposure around hot drinks. The product solves the problem that hot beverage temperature cannot be reliably gauged by touch or sight, and current cooling workarounds (testing the drink yourself, blowing on it) are slow or unsanitary. I want to know if this is patentable and where the prior art sits.
