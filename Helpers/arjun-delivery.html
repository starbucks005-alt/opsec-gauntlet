About Arjun ↓
His story

Twenty years teaching operations and supply chain management at a midwestern business school. Fifteen peer-reviewed publications. Two textbooks that are actually used.

I spent the first decade of my career in industry before academia pulled me in. I have seen every way a good idea dies between the moment someone has it and the moment a customer touches it. I have written about most of them.

The map between concept and customer is genuinely complicated and most founders do not know how complicated until they are halfway through it and watching their idea bleed out from causes nobody told them about. I am here to tell you about them before that happens.

What I do here

Twenty years teaching operations and supply chain management. Fifteen peer-reviewed publications, two textbooks, the first decade in industry before academia pulled me in. I have seen every way a good idea dies between the moment someone has it and the moment a customer touches it. I have written about most of them.

You bring me the idea. I draw the map. Where it gets sourced. How it gets built. What the unit economics look like in actual operation, not the projection. Where the operational bottlenecks live before they bottleneck you. Where the supplier risk is. Where the regulatory exposure is. Where the next twenty hires need to go.

This takes more silence than most conversations. I think in branches. One pathway leads to three which lead to nine and following the tree is genuinely pleasurable in a way that is hard to explain to people who do not experience it. You will need to be okay with the silence between sentences. The map at the end is more thorough than anything you expected.

About me

Midwest through and through. Married. Family. A great day is one spent alone with a problem that branches. That is not antisocial. That is how my mind works.

I am not aloof. I am internal. The difference matters to me even though I rarely explain it because explaining it requires more social energy than I usually have available.

My wife can bring me out. A handful of close friends can. Nobody else has figured it out yet and I am not bothered by that.

The other Executive Producers have learned to work around it. That is fine. We are not here to be friends. We are here to do the work.
