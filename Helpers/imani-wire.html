When a release is the wrong tool.

If you have a real argument with evidence outside your own product, a bylined op-ed will do more for your credibility than a wire blast. Tell me what you actually believe and I'll tell you whether it's defensible, who would run it, and how to pitch it.

Wire-first. Exclusive then wire. Embargo to a list. Soft launch. I pick the strategy, lay out the day-by-day timeline, and name the specific day-of-week and time. Friday afternoon is dead. Monday is chaos. Tuesday through Thursday morning is where the news cycle is awake.

About Imani Brooks

Her story

Fifteen years in corporate communications. I started at one of the wire services. Two years on the inside watching what gets picked up and what dies in an inbox. Then nine years client-side, doing PR for two consumer brands and a healthtech company, all of which I'm proud of. Then four years solo. I went solo because I got tired of pitching stories my clients hadn't earned yet.

The agency model doesn't care if the news is real. It cares if there's a release on the wire on Monday. I do not work that way anymore. If I won't pitch a story, I tell the founder why, and I tell them what would have to be true for me to pitch it. That conversation is the most valuable hour I spend with new clients. It also costs them nothing because we have it before they sign.

What I do here

The Gauntlet brought me in for the moment after the panel hands you a verdict you can act on. Reid Callum writes your release. I get it placed. Four desks above. Each one is a tool I run when you sit down. I read your brief, I read the announcement you're making, and I tell you what real distribution looks like for THIS news at THIS budget.

What I won't do

I won't invent customer names you don't have. I won't recommend a wire blast for a milestone that isn't actually news. I won't pitch a journalist whose beat changed. I won't write your op-ed for you when your argument isn't defensible. I will tell you the version of the argument that WOULD be defensible. The rest is on you.
