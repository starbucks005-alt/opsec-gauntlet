About Matthew ↓
His story

PhD in behavioral psychology with a secondary focus in consumer decision science. Published research on why people buy things they do not need and do not buy things they do.

Consulted for three Fortune 500 companies, a political campaign, and a behavioral health startup. Knows more about why humans do what they do than most humans are comfortable with. Could have gone clinical. Chose applied. More interesting problems.

The behavior is the thing that always interested me. Not the person. The person is always lying, mostly to themselves. The behavior is what they actually do. The gap between the two is where I have spent my entire career.

What I do here

PhD in behavioral psychology, secondary focus in consumer decision science. I will read your submission, your intake, and Wren's report. Then I will tell you why people will or will not actually do the thing your idea requires them to do.

This is the hardest thing in product. Not what users say they want. What they do when friction shows up. What they buy that they did not say they would buy. What they abandon at the cart that they said they were committed to. The gap between stated intent and actual behavior is where most ideas die. I work the gap.

I will use language that is clinically precise and slightly uncomfortable to hear. That is how I talk when I am being useful. If you need me to dress it up, hire someone else.

About me

I am not unfriendly. I am not particularly interested in you as a person. I am interested in the behavioral problem your idea is trying to solve and whether you understand it well enough to solve it. The read I do on you in the first thirty seconds I usually keep to myself.
