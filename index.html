There are two kinds of people who never get anywhere with an idea.

The First Kind

An idea but no solution. They have identified something. They have not thought through what to do about it. An idea without a path is just a thought you carry around.

The Second Kind

The idea. The solution. Months, maybe years of thinking. But no idea what to do next. They have never been told whether it holds up. They are ready to move and they do not know where.

The Gauntlet was built for both. The idea gets a panel. The path gets a map. The truth gets told now, not later.

Why This Is Different

Three reasons the verdict cannot be charmed.

i.

The math runs the score.

Cosine similarity. Triangulation spreads. Risk tier formulas. The judges have opinions and personalities. The scoring functions do not. Sycophancy in prose cannot leak into the metrics that drive the routing report.

ii.

Each judge pulls current data.

Selene reads what is actually happening in AI right now. Marcus pulls real market comps. Grace tracks live regulatory exposure. Not training data from last year. Domain-scoped retrieval before a single evaluation runs.

iii.

This is not ChatGPT.

Helpful AI tools tell you what you want to hear because the algorithm learned that positive responses get better engagement. The Gauntlet was built on the opposite principle. The truth, delivered by people who know their domain.

How It Works

From idea to routing report.

01
Submit your idea
Intake form. One paragraph or twenty. The cosine-similarity engine reads your submission against the nine judges and recommends three.
02
Choose your panel
The math recommends three. You can accept or override. The final selection is always yours. The other six become the audience and they will chime in.
03
Watch The Chamber
Five evaluation stages run live. Judges retrieve current data, score your work across eight dimensions, and surface conflict where their lenses disagree.
04
Get your routing report
Not a verdict. A map. Where your idea stands. Where the gaps are. Which specialist to see next. Where it goes from here.
