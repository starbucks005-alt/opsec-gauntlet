§ Critical Infrastructure Review Panel

Meet the Judges

When you enter the OPSEC Gauntlet, you are not pitching to influencers or business coaches. You are presenting your idea to a panel of national security specialists who evaluate it the way a real threat analyst would.

Each judge represents a critical infrastructure sector. Each one brings a different risk model, a different operational lens, and a different threshold for what counts as a real vulnerability. The panel's assessments reflect real-world considerations used across federal, state, and private-sector security programs.

The OPSEC Gauntlet is evaluated by a multidisciplinary panel of subject-matter experts representing the full range of United States critical infrastructure sectors. These judges apply established risk frameworks, operational standards, and sector-specific threat models to assess the resilience, feasibility, and security posture of each submitted concept.

They do not flatter. They do not soften. They do not guess. They tell you whether your idea survives contact with the real world.

§ Panel Function

The OPSEC Gauntlet judges operate as an independent review body. Their evaluations identify operational gaps, sector-specific risks, and potential failure points. The panel does not provide legal advice, operational directives, or classified assessments. Their role is to provide structured, sector-informed analysis to support decision-making and risk awareness. Completion of the Gauntlet does not imply certification, endorsement, or approval. It indicates that the idea has undergone structured OPSEC review.
